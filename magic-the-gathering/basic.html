Basic Magic the Gathering Rules

Play Area

The placement of the cards sets the stage. There are 6 areas in the game.

  • Library
    • The library is where you draw your cards. They remain facedown. If you do not have any cards in your library and you are forced to draw a card, you lose the game.
  • Hand
    • For most of the game, unless a card says otherwise, you can only cast spells from your hand. At the beginning of every game, you draw seven cards. This makes up your starting hand. At the end of your turn, if you have more than seven cards, you must discard down to have only seven cards maximum. The discard card(s) go into your graveyard.
  • Battlefield
    • Cards that go on the battlefield are called permanets. This includes lands, creatures, artifacts, enchantments, and planeswalkers. Instants and soceries are never on the battlefield. The battlefield can be arranged to each player's personal preference. It is usually best to keep your lands/mana at the bottom of the battlefield and group your other cards by type.
  • Graveyard
    • The graveyard is your discard pile. When a creature dies, enchantment or artifact are destroyed they go into the graveyard. Once you cast an instant or sorcery, they also are discarded and are put into the graveyard.
  • Exile
    • Some spells and abilities can exile cards. When a card is exiled, it is set apart from the game.
  • Command Zone
    • When playing commander format, the commander of the deck gets placed in the command zone at the beginning of the game. if this creature were to die, be exiled, or returned to your hand you may chose to place them back in the command zone. Each time they re-enter the command zone, there is a commander tax of two mana for each time they have been casted previously.

    Game Actions

  • Tapping/Untapping
    • To tap a card means to turn it on its side. This shows when a card has been used for the turn. Whenever you use lands to create mana, the lands become tapped. Whenever a creature attacks, the creature becomes tapped as well. Some activated abilities will also specify if they need to be tapped in order to activate the ability. Permanets cannot become untapped unless it is during your untap step.
  • Casting Spells
    • In order to cast spells, you must pay the mana cost. The mana cost is in the top right hand corner of the card. Lands are used as specific mana. Each land has a symbol for black, red, blue, green, and white. You must use the correct corresponding colors to cast the spells. When a card does not show a mana symbol and instead shows a number, this means you can tap any color of mana for that cost. These are known as colorless mana costs.
  • Attacking/Blocking
    • The most common way to win is by attacking with creatures. If a creature attacks and the opponet does not block, it deals combat damage to the player. If damage is dealt to an opponent, they lose that much life.
  • Targeting
    • Some spells use the word "target". This means they must chose a specific permanets for the ability to target.

    Parts of the Turn

    Beginning Phase

  • Untap
    • If there are any permanets that are tapped, you would untap them.
  • Upkeep
    • within upkeep, a player can cast instants and activate abilities in which cards will have the wording, "at the beginning of your upkeep"
  • Draw
    • You must draw a card from your library. If there are no cards in your library during your draw step, you lose the game.

    First Main Phase

    You can cast as many sorceries, instants, creatures, enchantments, planeswalkers, and artifacts. You can also activate abilites. Each turn you can also play one land. Each player can only play one land per turn. Opponents can also cast instants and activate abilites during your main phase.

    Combat Phase

  • Beginning of Combat
    • Players can cast instants and activate abilites.
  • Declare Attackers
    • You can decide which of your untapped creatures will attack and which player they will attack. When a creature attacks, It gets tapped unless it says otherwise. If a creature was just cast during the first main phase, it has summoning sickness and cannot attack unless it says otherwise.
  • Declare Blockers
    • Your opponents you have chose to attack can now decide if any of their untapped creatures will block your attacking creatures. Multiple creatures can block a single attacker, and the attacking player decides which order to receive damage. Players can also cast instants and activate abilities.
  • Combat Damage
    • Each attacking or blocking creature that is still on the battlefield assings combat damage to the defending player. All combat damage is dealt at the same time. Instants and activated abilities can be played during this time.
  • End of Combat
    • Players can cast instants and activate abilities.

    Second Main Phase

    This phase is the same as the first main phase however if you played a land during the first main phase, you cannot play one during the second.

    Ending Phase

  • End Step
    • Some cards have abilities taht state "at the beginning of your end step". Players can also cast instants and abilities.
  • Cleanup Step
    • All damange on creatures is removed and all "until end of turn" effects end. If you have more than seven cards in your hand, you may chose which ones to discard until you only have seven. No one can cast or activate abilities during this step.
