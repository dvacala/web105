Overview

Magic the Gathering is a card game which is played by two or more players who engage in a battle acting as wizards called planeswalkers. Each player has their own deck which they have made or have purchased preconstructed. These decks follow the rules of the format in which they're being played. A few of the most popular formats include:

  • Standard
  • Commander
  • Booster Draft

We will be focusing on the Commander format. Within this format, each deck is made up of 100 cards. Commander is about chosing a legandary creature that will serve as your commander and the rest of your deck is built around them and their color identity.In Commander, each player starts out with 40 life instead of 20. Color Types

There are five different color types. Each card can be a mono color or multi color. To cast creatures and spells, you must pay mana of the card's colors. These colors consist of the following:

  • Red
  • Blue
  • Black
  • White
  • Green

Text Box

In the text box of cards, some cards have special abilites. These abilities can be a caracteristic an object has, something that changes how the game affects that player or opponents, and/or can be activated/triggered. Abilites can be beneficial or detrimental.

Power/Toughness

On each creature card, there is a power and toughness that is displayed (Example: 2/2). The first number is the creature's power. This represents the amount of damange it can deal during combat. The second number is the toughness. This is the amount of damange that needs to be dealt to it in a single turn to destroy it.

Card Types

There are different card types as well. Each card type is used in different ways to play the game. These cards consist of:

  • Creatures
    • These are permanent cards. They serve the player, usually by fighting on their behalf.
  • Artifacts
    • These are permanet cards. They represent magical items.
  • Sorceries
    • Represents one shot spells. They are not permanets and are immediately put into the graveyard.
  • Instants
    • Like sorceries, they are one shot spells. They are not permanets. These however can be played at any time even during another player's turn.
  • Enchantments
    • Represents magical effects and are permanets. They usually remain in play indefinitely. Most have continous or triggered abilites.
  • Lands
    • Represents locations under player's control. Most of which generate mana to cast creatures and spells.
  • Planeswalkers
    • Within each game, players are considered Planeswakers. There are cards however that are also Planeswakers which can have abilities that are activated with loyalty counters.
